Why Qualified Leads Stopped Converting

Inbound lead analysis · 33,076 leads · 30/60/90 day action plan

Context

An HR SaaS platform watched its MQL-to-SAL conversion rate slide and wanted to know why. I analysed 33,076 inbound leads to trace where the pipeline was breaking and what could be done about it.

Lead volume had spiked 3,270% from December 2022 to January 2023, from 40 to 2,703 leads a month, and conversion fell in lockstep and never recovered. Three root causes stood out: capacity constraints on the SDR team, data quality issues at the point of capture, and weak lead qualification. I set out fixes across 30, 60, and 90 day horizons.

1. The Volume Spike Broke the Pipeline

Lead volume jumped from 40 in December 2022 to 2,703 in January 2023, and remained elevated through the analysis period. The MQL-to-SAL conversion rate declined correspondingly, becoming more pronounced from June to August 2023.

Lead volume vs MQL-to-SAL conversion rate (2023)

Monthly volumes and January's 96% conversion rate are computed from the data. The conversion line for subsequent months is directional, illustrating the sustained decline through to August. August volume reflects a partial month.

Key signal: Despite the massive volume increase, January 2023 still maintained a 96% conversion rate, suggesting initial sales management of the pipeline. The sustained decline in subsequent months indicates capacity constraints catching up.

A point-in-time check revealed 1,342 leads with status marked as "New" that had not been actioned. The trend of unconverted leads roughly mirrors the overall volume spike, signalling that the team could not process leads at the rate they were arriving.

2. More Volume Brought More Waste

The top reason for lead loss: unqualified leads. The trend mirrors the volume spike from early 2023. Breaking down the sub-reasons reveals structural issues in the pipeline.

Lead loss reasons breakdown

Indicative composition. Verified anchors from the data: unqualified leads are the top loss reason, 15% of lost leads had invalid contact information, and 875 losses carried no documented reason at all.

Data quality at the source

  • 15% of lost leads were due to invalid contact information (incorrect or outdated phone/email)
  • Disqualified leads were disproportionately related to client size mismatch, suggesting a need for earlier segmentation visibility
  • 875 lost leads had no documented loss reason at all
Key finding: The volume spike did not just add more leads. It changed the composition of the pipeline, introducing a higher proportion of leads that could never convert. Without front-end filtering, the SDR team was spending time on leads that should never have reached them.

3. Capacity Was Evenly Stretched, Not Unevenly Distributed

The top 8 lead owners each managed 3.9% to 4.7% of 2023 volume, a relatively even distribution. This rules out a single bottleneck owner as the cause. The problem is systemic: everyone is overloaded equally.

4. Root Cause Assessment

Process and capacity constraints

  • SLA compliance gaps: 27% of MQLs exceed the 2-day follow-up threshold, contributing to lead aging
  • Volume-driven deterioration: the 2023 spike strained SDR capacity, reducing contact attempt quality
  • Unclear acceptance criteria: SAL conversion standards lack auditability

System issues

  • Conversion rate masking: low MQL-to-SAL conversion may artificially inflate win rates, obscuring pipeline health
  • Attribution gaps: Originating Lead ID and Lead ID fields show no matches between datasets, preventing opportunity-level analysis

Data quality issues

  • Insufficient sub-categorisation: "Disqualified" lacks granularity on customer segmentation
  • No validation at point of capture: no standardised checks for phone (E.164) or email (MX/DNS)
  • Undocumented losses: 875 leads lost with no recorded reason

5. Recommended Action Plan

Implementation timeline: actions by horizon

30 days: immediate stabilisation

  • Data quality enforcement: implement form-level validation (E.164 phone, MX/DNS email checks, SMTP verification)
  • Mandate critical fields: title, function, region, company size for segmentation
  • Deploy enrichment layer: append industry, employee count, HQ country, tech stack to prioritise high-value leads
  • Segmentation-based routing: create a high-priority queue for enterprise segment leads
  • Monitoring: daily MQL-to-SAL dashboard by segment, owner, and SLA compliance with automated alerts

60 days: process optimisation

  • Enforce 1-2 hour MQL-to-SDR SLA with manager escalation
  • Audit lead assignment rules and field mapping logic
  • Analyse correlation between response time, contact attempts, and conversion
  • Implement mandatory loss sub-reasons for all dispositioned leads

90 days: strategic alignment

  • Review sales compensation to balance conversion rate alongside win rate
  • Analyse lead owner distribution for workload equity and skill-based routing
  • Establish closed-loop feedback from Sales to Marketing on lead quality by source and campaign

6. Methodology

  • Dataset: 33,076 leads with MQL and SAL timestamps, lead status, loss reasons, and owner assignments
  • Missing SAL dates were treated as leads not yet converted to SAL
  • August 2023 data appears incomplete (dataset maximum date is early August); trend analysis accounts for this
  • Opportunities dataset was excluded due to lack of linkage between Lead ID and Originating Lead ID fields. Resolving this mapping would enable revenue impact quantification.
